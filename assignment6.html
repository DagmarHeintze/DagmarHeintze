I find that DPP membership is the single strongest predictor for voting for president Tsai. All other variables, a voter being female, a higher level of education, income and a higher age all lead to a decrease in likelihood of voting for the president. Membership in the KMT party, furthermore, has a strong, negative and significant effect on the likelihood of a person voting for president Tsai.

When testing two alternative model specifications, I find that DPP membership is still a strong and statistically significant predictor for voting for president Tsai. Furthermore, support for Taiwan’s independence, a negative view of the economy, and a person self-identifying as Taiwanese are all positive and statistically significant predictors for a voter electing the president. A voter being a descendant from mainland China is a negative and statistically significant predictor for voting for president Tsai.
